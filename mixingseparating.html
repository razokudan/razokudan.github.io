Mixing and separation of substances

Mixing and separating substances is part of physics, as the substances do not transform into a new substances. Nonetheless mixtures are part of chemical experiments.

Mixing

Solutions

A solution consists of at least one solvent in which the solutes are getting diluted into. The solutes will liquefy if they were solids. The substance with the greatest amount in the solution usually is the solvent.

  • A homogeneous solution has one phase, i.e. it appears uniformly.
  • A heterogeneous solution has at least two phases, i.e. at least one solute e.g., settles out as a sediment.
Suspensions

A fluid (external phase) containing solid particles (internal phase)is a suspension. Thus, as the particles are generally visible, a suspension is always heterogeneous. Eventually the particles will settle out.

Suspensions and colloids are grouped as a dispersion.

Colloids

A colloid is like a suspension, but with an internal phase made up of solids in the nano- to micrometer range. Colloids go by various names depending on the external phase, e.g., aerosol is a liquid/solid in gas, see table below. It seems a complex topic, please refer to Wikipedia for more information.

Suspensions and colloids are grouped as a dispersion.

  • colloid (foam (fine like cream))
  • suspension (foam (coarse like sea foam))
  • solution (beer etc. (alcohol is soluble in water))
  • emulsion (cosmetic cream)

  • emulsions will turn into suspensions if no emulsifier is added.
  • solution (seawater (salt in water))

  • suspension (mud)
solid
  • solution (H in metal (hydroxides))
  • foam (aerogel)
  • foam (sponge, dry)
  • solution (paraffin wax)

  • suspension (wet sponge)
  • solution (alloy)
  • sol (cranberry glass)
  • suspension (clay)
Remember that suspensions and colloids are both a kind of dispersion. So colloids are a fine dispersion and suspensions are a coarse dispersion.
