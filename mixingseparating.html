Mixing and separating of substances

Mixing substances is part of physics, as the substances do not combine to transform into a new substance. Nonetheless mixtures are part of chemical experiments.

The three forms of mixtures:

Solutions

A solution consists of at least one solvent in which the solutes are getting diluted into. The solutes will liquefy if they were solids. The substance with the greatest amount in the solution usually is the solvent.

A homogeneous solution has one phase, i.e. it appears uniformly.

A heterogeneous solution has at least two phases, i.e. at least one solute e.g., settles out as a sediment.

Suspensions

A fluid (external phase) containing solid particles (internal phase)is a suspension. Thus, as the particles are generally visible, a suspension is always heterogeneous. Eventually the particles will settle out.

Suspensions and colloids are grouped as a dispersion.

Colloids

A colloid is like a suspension, but with an internal phase made up of solids in the nano- to micrometer range.

Suspensions and colloids are grouped as a dispersion.
